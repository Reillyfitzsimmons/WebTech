It's Monday morning, you're at school, but we all know you're not really at school.

You might as well be in bed because that's all your brain is thinking about- sleep. You're tired, groggy, confused, and the last thing you need is someone telling you your shirt is on inside out. Why does this happen?

According to Hank Green, the reason is because of your circadian rhythm. Your circadian rhythm is your body's natural clock that coincides with the light of day and darkness of night. Virtually evey single organism has a circadian rhythm, but most humans aren't using it to their advantage.

Instead of going to bed the same time evey night and waking up at the same time every morning (yes, including weekends) we choose to stay up and have a few more drinks with our friends. Not only is this unnatural, but it means you could be missing out on crucial time in the mornings.
