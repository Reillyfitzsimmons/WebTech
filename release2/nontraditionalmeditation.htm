Doing the dishes, writing, journaling, and walking are all forms of meditation that people today don't usually think of as being "meditation." Accoring to Dr. Judson Brewer (a psychiatrist, neuroscientist, and New York Times Bestselling author), the key to meditation is awareness. One of the keys to forming habits is being aware of when you are suseptible to the behavior you are looking to reduce. For example, if you have been feeling anxious, or have an anxiety disorder, focusing on why you feel that way and what you are doing when you feel that way might prove beneficial to how you function. If you are a smoker and want to stop smoking, Dr. Brewer suggest that being aware of how a cigarette actually tastes.
